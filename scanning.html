3D-printed object.

The third project of the semester was to design a 3D-printable object such that it would not be possible to produce it using subractive manufacturing. The 3D-modeling software used, as in the previous project, was Fusion 360.

During the course of this project I utilized two programs in order to sufficiently complete the task at hand: Fusion 360 and Cura.

Working on this project, I already had some experience with Fusion 360 from the last project. This eneabled me to immediately start working on my model. I searched online for inspiration and came across this neat Nespresso capsule dispenser, which I decided on designing and printing.

I started with a simple sketch and extruded it upwards to my liking.

I then created a sketch on top of my extrusion and used it to cut out a majority of the objects insides.

In order to make the model a bit lighter, I cut out unnecessary and excess material out throughout the making of the model. This is one example.

This is another example of weigth reduction.

Here I am making the slot for the capsules. I start with a sketch on the object's side, which is created according to my measurements and estimates. Next, I cut.

These extrusions create a small ledge for the box to sit on and let the capsules drop to the bottom. This is exactly the same on both sides.

After I had created the ledges, I completely cut out the bottom of the inside. I did this to be able to more accurately sketch the actual bottom of the holder, which can be seen below.

I made some measurements, sketched the bottom to my liking and then extruded it across.

As a guidance for the capsules I, again, did some careful calculations and sketched the result on the inside of one wall. I then extruded the sketch across to the other wall.

I decided to make a stand for the holder to be more stable and allows me to connect two holders. These are the slots which are to slide in my stand.

Again, to reduce weight I made a sketch on one side of the object, which, when happy with, I then cut out to the other side.

To further reduce weight!

This is the final outcome of the holder.

As I mentioned before, I decided to make a stand for the holder(s). I obviously went by the measurements of the holder I had modeled and decided on a desireble thickeness.

I started by sketching a simple rectangle according to my measurements and extruded it.

On top of the object I created a sketch and cut out a part of the sections for the holders.

On top of the holders' sections I sketched the insertions matching the cut outs I had made on the holder before, however, allowing for about 0.5mm leeway.

I then created a sketch on one side and cut out to the other to angle them.

This is the final outcome of the stand. On both the holder and the stand I made 1mm chamfers on most edges.
